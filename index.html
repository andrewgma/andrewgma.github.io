Here are some projects I have worked on:

Predicting Bitcoin Prices using Time Series
Project for Statistics 434 (Time Series) at Penn.
I use time series models to predict Bitcoin prices with some success. I also demonstrate a basic trading scheme to show opportunities that exist in Bitcoin exchanges (see handout).
Marketing/Statistics Paper
Project for Statistics 476 (Applied Probability Models in Marketing) at Penn.
I use probability models to forecast lifetime visits based on visitor frequency studies at Yosemite National Park. I tell a story about Yosemite's "visitor markets" and differences in visitor behavior inferred by the model.
Breadcrumbs
An app that points you to your friends in crowded, public places.
To use, open Breadcrumbs on a GPS enabled smartphone. Enter your name and friend's phone number. Your friend receives a text message containing a link to Breadcrumbs. This page displays an arrow pointing to you.
I built the back end using Node.js and WebSockets.
