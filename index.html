Welcome. I graduated from the University of Pennsylvania in 2013 with dual degrees in mathematics and business from the College of Arts & Sciences and the Wharton School.

Here are some projects I have worked on:

Breadcrumbs
An app that points you to your friends in crowded, public places.
To use, open Breadcrumbs on a GPS enabled smartphone. Enter your name and friend's phone number. Your friend receives a text message containing a link to Breadcrumbs. This page displays an arrow pointing to you.
I built the back end using Node.js and WebSockets.
Beets JamFinder
Tagline: Discover new music, playing live near you tonight. Embedded SoundCloud player lets you sample the artist's jams. For web and iOS.
Built in under 40 hours with a team of four at PennApps Spring 2013. I built the front end with HTML, CSS, and JavaScript.
We won the prize for "Best use of a Mashery API." (app is not currently live)
Quick Refills
The easiest way to refill your CVS prescription drugs.
Enter your Rx numbers, CVS store phone number, and Quick Refills will dial your store's automated phone system and order your refill.

Marketing/Statistics Paper
Project for Statistics 476 (Applied Probability Models in Marketing) at Penn.
I use probability models to forecast lifetime visits based on visitor frequency studies at Yosemite National Park. I tell a story about Yosemite's "visitor markets" and differences in visitor behavior inferred by the model.
