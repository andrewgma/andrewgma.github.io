In the past, I interned at MarkLogic and Lab126, Amazon's Kindle R&D lab.

Here are some projects I have worked on:

Predicting Bitcoin Prices using Time Series
Project for Statistics 434 (Time Series) at Penn.
We use time series models to predict Bitcoin prices with some success. We also demonstrate a basic trading scheme to show opportunities that exist in Bitcoin exchanges (see handout).
Marketing/Statistics Paper
Project for Statistics 476 (Applied Probability Models in Marketing) at Penn.
I use probability models to forecast lifetime visits based on visitor frequency studies at Yosemite National Park. I tell a story about Yosemite's "visitor markets" and differences in visitor behavior inferred by the model.
Research in Data Mining at Penn
Research assistant for professors in Computer Science, Statistics, and Linguistics.
I wrote Python and MATLAB scripts to apply canonical correlation analysis and other statistical methods on a data set from Wine Spectator Magazine.
